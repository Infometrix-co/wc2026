Disclosures

What is locked, what is exploratory, and what is a coin flip

The goals lens is exploratory. Act 1 — conversion — and Act 3 — goals per euro — were not among the pre-registered questions. The pre-registered sections are the parts of this page that were falsifiable in advance.

Everything quarter-final is exploratory too. QF pre-registration was not frozen in time — the next freeze landed only before the semi-finals — so every figure derived from matches 97–100 (the results reads, “Does the ball win?”, the money board) is exploratory by our own rule and labelled as such where it appears.

Late-goal accounting. A late goal is any regulation goal from the 80th minute onward — stoppage time included, extra time excluded — reported as a share of goals, never as a per-minute claim. Four minute-90 goal clusters are order-ambiguous in the source feed; the leading-bucket majority holds under all 48 possible orderings.

Assigned vs measured archetypes. The archetype colours throughout show assigned labels, fixed before the tournament. Measured group-stage signatures disagree with the assignments in 52% of cases (25 of 48). That includes Algeria, whose Counter Kings designation is an editorial lock contradicted by its measured signature — 63% possession and +0.09 xG difference per match make it a structured, near-neutral side. We keep the lock and disclose it; quietly reclassifying a team after seeing its results is how frameworks stop being falsifiable.

Penalties are a coin flip. 4 of the 28 knockout ties so far were decided from the spot. A shootout carries almost no information about the 120 minutes before it, which is why every pens result is flagged wherever it appears.

How we know these numbers are trustworthy

FIFA
Match results — used to spot-check every recovered scoreline before it entered the database. R32 results corroborated against API-Football's independent fixture feed (goals and shootouts, 16/16 match); stats spot-checked against FIFA match-centre counting stats.
The Athletic
Group standings (the checksum that verified all 72 results) and the tactical guides behind the archetype language.
Transfermarkt
Squad market values, all 48 nations, single June-28 snapshot.
API-Football
Per-match xG, possession, shots and passing — 144 team-match records across the 72 group games, plus 56 across the knockout rounds — and 1588 timed match events behind the late-goal accounting.

Reading the numbers

xG (expected goals)
The scoring probability of each shot, summed. Measures the quality of chances created, independent of finishing luck.
xG difference
xG created minus xG conceded, per match. Positive means the team dominated the chances — the piece's dominance axis.
Squad market value
Sum of the squad's Transfermarkt valuations: the market's collective estimate of talent — the money axis.
Value residual
A team's xG difference minus what its budget predicts. Positive means it beat its price — the style signal once money is stripped out.

Method. The dataset was assembled through an AI coding agent under a strict gate discipline — inspect, dry-run, verify, then write — with every database change backed up and read-back-verified. The discipline caught what trusting the output would not have: 23 reversed scorelines, a value set silently truncated to only the teams that advanced (which would have made "money predicts advancement" a tautology), and null card-counts that would have quietly poisoned the averages. An agent's confident output is a draft, not a fact; the analyst's value is designing the verification. The knockout dataset was built under the same gate discipline — results parsed programmatically from the published bracket, penalty mappings hard-asserted against externally verified values.
